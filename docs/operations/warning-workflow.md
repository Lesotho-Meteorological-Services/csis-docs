# Warning Workflow

This section describes the operational workflow for warning generation, review, authorization, dissemination, and post-event recordkeeping in CSIS.

It is intended for operational users responsible for hazard monitoring, warning preparation, approval, and public communication.

## Before you begin

Before starting a warning workflow, confirm that:

- the relevant monitoring data and observations are available
- threshold conditions or trigger criteria have been assessed
- the responsible forecaster or duty officer is assigned
- dissemination channels and contact lists are current
- the escalation path for approval is understood

## Trigger a warning workflow

Role: `Forecaster` or `Duty Officer`

Use this procedure when observed or forecast conditions indicate that a warning product may be required.

1. Review the latest forecast guidance, observations, and impact indicators.
2. Confirm that conditions meet the internal criteria for initiating a warning workflow.
3. Open the appropriate workspace or folder in the CSIS Collaboration Interface.
4. Create a new warning product using the approved warning template.
5. Record the hazard type, affected area, expected timing, and severity level.
6. Save the draft so that the warning can proceed to quality review.

## Draft and quality check the warning

Role: `Forecaster`

Use this procedure to prepare the warning content and validate it before authorization.

1. Complete the warning message using clear and concise operational language.
2. Confirm that the warning identifies the hazard, timing, location, and expected impacts.
3. Check that protective advice or action guidance is included where required.
4. Review the message for consistency with supporting evidence and maps.
5. Conduct an internal quality check for spelling, clarity, and formatting.
6. Save the updated draft and prepare it for authorization.

> Screenshot placeholder: Warning draft showing hazard summary, affected area, timing, and action guidance fields completed.

### Quality check focus

Verify that:

- the warning level matches the severity of the expected event
- affected districts or regions are correctly identified
- times and dates are explicit and operationally accurate
- wording is clear for both technical partners and public-facing dissemination

## Authorize the warning

Role: `Head of Operations/Director`

Use this procedure to review and authorize the warning for release.

1. Open the submitted warning draft from notifications or the assigned operational folder.
2. Review the warning content, supporting evidence, and stated impacts.
3. Confirm that the hazard level and geographic coverage are appropriate.
4. Approve the warning for release, or return it for revision if clarification is required.
5. Ensure the product status reflects the authorization decision.

> Screenshot placeholder: Warning review screen showing the approval decision controls and current product status.

## Disseminate the warning

Role: `Forecaster`, `Dissemination Officer`, or assigned communications staff

Use this procedure after authorization has been completed.

1. Open the approved warning product.
2. Confirm that the latest approved version is the one selected for release.
3. Publish or distribute the warning through the required dissemination channels.
4. Verify that intended recipients or systems have received the warning.
5. Record the dissemination time and channels used.

> Screenshot placeholder: Approved warning ready for release with dissemination options visible.

## Complete post-event review and recordkeeping

Role: `Operational Team`

Use this procedure after dissemination and event monitoring have been completed.

1. Confirm that the final warning record is stored in the correct operational location.
2. Capture key details such as issue time, affected area, severity, and dissemination channels.
3. Record any updates, cancellations, or follow-up advisories linked to the warning.
4. Document operational lessons, communication issues, or notable outcomes.
5. Retain the warning and its supporting records for audit, review, and training purposes.

## Good practice

- Use warning templates consistently across all hazard products.
- Keep language direct, precise, and aligned with approved terminology.
- Avoid issuing a warning before severity, timing, and area have been checked.
- Record every approval and dissemination action to support traceability.
- Review warning performance after significant events to improve future operations.
